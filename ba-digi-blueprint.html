The Strategic Idea
If their own site looks this good, imagine what they'd build for you.

Most marketing agency websites claim creativity but look identical. BA Digi's site silently demonstrates capability through its own execution. Every pixel is proof of craft. Seven services grouped into three pillars that tell one story: Build it. Grow it. Launch it.

Visitor Psychology
Four Beliefs Before They Pay

Every visitor lands with skepticism. Within 60 seconds, the site must advance these four beliefs. If a section doesn't serve one of them, it doesn't belong.

01

They're good at what they do. The site itself proves this — the design, the interactions, the information architecture. Capability demonstrated, not claimed.

02

They have a structured approach. Not making it up as they go. Three pillars, clear process, defined methodology. This is a studio, not a freelancer.

03

Their pricing is clear and within reach. No "contact us for a quote" mystery. Tiers are visible, transparent, and structured. The visitor can self-qualify.

04

Getting started is simple and low-pressure. Multiple contact paths, no hard sell, configurator quiz for the undecided. The door is open, not guarded.

Site Architecture
Four Pages. Each With a Job.

Every page advances the argument. No filler. A structured funnel that moves visitors from curiosity to conversion.

Page 01

Home

The Hook

Makes the visitor stay. Demonstrates capability within 5 seconds. Previews everything — pillars, proof, packages — but goes deep on none. Creates doors to the other three pages.

Page 02

Services

The Depth

Answers "can you do what I specifically need?" Walks through Build, Grow, Launch with enough detail to feel comprehensive. Shows the process — how BA Digi works start to finish.

Page 03

Packages

The Decision

Where browsing becomes buying. Clear tiers, clear pricing, clear scope. The configurator quiz helps the undecided find their tier and captures lead data before they even reach Contact.

Page 04

Contact

The Conversion

By now, they've decided to talk. Zero friction. Email, phone, WhatsApp, form. Configurator context pre-filled. The page feels like opening a door, not filling out an application.

01
Home

The showpiece. One job: make the visitor stay. It doesn't sell — it demonstrates. The emotional arc: Impress → Educate → Prove → Differentiate → Tease → Convert.

Section 1
Hero
"This agency is different — polished, confident, clearly good at design."
Headline: "Digital Marketing & Creative Services Built to Perform." — not generic "We're a creative agency."

Subtext: "Website development, marketing campaigns, and creative design — engineered for businesses that take growth seriously."

Two CTAs: "Get a Proposal" (copper, primary — ready buyer) and "View Packages" (outlined — researcher).

Trust bar: Three micro-promises: ◆ Strategy-first approach · ◆ Transparent pricing · ◆ End-to-end delivery.

Visual element (right side): 2–3 overlapping dark browser frames at slight angles showing premium websites — built with CSS, not images. Silently says "we build websites."
→ Connects to: Pillars section below — hero makes them curious, pillars explain the methodology.
Section 2
The Three Pillars
"They have a structured methodology: Build, Grow, Launch."
Intro: "One Agency. Three Disciplines." — "From the first pixel to the last campaign report."

Three tall cards side by side, each with a colored accent line:
Build
Create the assets
Grow
Drive the results
Launch
Orchestrate the play
Each card lists its 2–3 services beneath the tagline. Each links to its section on the Services page.
Most agencies list services in a flat grid — "SEO, Web Design, PPC..." Grouping into pillars creates a journey, not a menu. The client thinks "they build it, grow it, then launch campaigns."
→ Connects to: Services page (deep dive) and Proof section below.
Section 3
Proof of Craft
"Their work quality is visually evident — I can see it."
A staggered grid of 4–6 work preview cards. Each card shows a type label ("Website", "Campaign", "Social", "Presentation") inside a dark styled frame with CSS texture. Not fake screenshots — the visual design of the cards themselves demonstrates capability.

Closing line below: "Every project is a proof of concept. For what we'd build for you."

Small note: "Portfolio pieces added with your approval." — tells Daniel we need his work samples, without breaking the visitor's experience.
The site itself IS the proof. Placeholder frames look intentional — like a gallery waiting for work. Real pieces get added as Daniel completes projects. The section is architecturally ready.
→ Connects to: Why BA Digi section — from "look at what we do" to "here's why we're different."
Section 4
Why BA Digi
"Working with them means clarity, structure, and focus."
Two-column layout. Left: 4 differentiator rows —
  • Strategy before execution. — We don't start building until the thinking is done.
  • Transparent, structured pricing. — No hourly billing surprises. Packages are fixed.
  • Full-stack delivery. — Design, development, marketing — one team, one relationship.
  • Built for growth, not busywork. — We focus on work that moves the needle.
Right panel (dark elevated surface, copper left border): "We're not a freelancer marketplace. We're not a volume agency. We're a focused creative studio that builds things properly."
The differentiators address the three fears every business owner has: "Will they waste my money?" (strategy first), "Will costs spiral?" (transparent pricing), "Will I manage multiple vendors?" (full-stack).
→ Connects to: Packages Preview — from "here's why we're different" to "here's what it costs."

02
Services

The depth page. Answers "can you do what I specifically need?" Arc: Orient → Deep Dive × 3 → Process → Convert.

Section 1
Hero
Shorter hero — 60–70vh, centered. Heading: "Build. Grow. Launch." — each word on its own line, in its pillar color (copper, emerald, cream). Large, bold, immediate orientation.
Subtext: "Three disciplines. One infrastructure. Every project follows this logic."
Section 2
BUILD Pillar
"They create digital assets with craft and intentionality."
Copper accent label. Heading: "We Create the Assets." Three service cards side by side:
  • Website Development — custom design, responsive, SEO foundations, performance optimized, CMS integration, analytics setup
  • Graphic Design — brand identity, social media assets, advertising creatives, print materials, style guides
  • Presentation Design — pitch decks, investor presentations, sales decks, corporate templates
Each card has an icon, description, and capabilities list showing specific deliverables.
Section 3
GROW Pillar
"Growth isn't guesswork — they use structured strategies."
Emerald accent label. Heading: "We Drive the Results." Three service cards:
  • Digital Marketing — strategy, content marketing, email campaigns, SEO, social media management, funnel design
  • Online Advertising — Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn, retargeting, A/B testing, conversion tracking
  • Traffic Generation — organic growth, referral systems, partnership-driven traffic, landing page optimisation
Section 4
LAUNCH Pillar
"Campaigns are coordinated strategic plays, not random ads."
Cream accent label. Heading: "We Orchestrate the Full Play." Single deep card — not three. Campaigns are the culmination of Build + Grow working together.

Marketing Campaigns: end-to-end strategy, creative development, multi-channel execution, budget management, performance tracking, post-campaign analysis.

Visual: a campaign flow diagram — Strategy → Creative → Launch → Optimise → Report
Presenting campaigns as one comprehensive offering — not three sub-services — positions it as the premium tier of what BA Digi does.
Section 5
How We Work
"The process is clear, repeatable, and designed for client clarity."
4-step horizontal timeline (desktop), vertical on mobile:
01
Discovery
We listen. Map the landscape before touching a pixel.
02
Strategy
Define approach, channels, timeline, budget. You approve first.
03
Execution
Build, launch, manage. Regular check-ins. Full visibility.
04
Results
Measure, report, refine. Clear picture of what was achieved.
Business owners burned by agencies love seeing a process. It says "we won't disappear after taking your money" without saying it.
Section 6
CTA
"Know what you need? Let's scope it." — Get a Proposal (copper) · View Packages (outlined).
03
Packages

The decision page. Where browsing becomes buying. Arc: Frame → Compare → Discover → Reassure → Convert.

Section 1
Hero
"Transparent Pricing. Structured Delivery." — "Every package is scoped, priced, and delivered with full clarity. No hourly surprises. No scope creep."
Section 2
Tier Cards
"There's a clear tier for my business size and need."
4 detailed cards — more detail than the Home preview. Each card includes tier name, price, target audience, 4–5 bullet points of what's included, and a CTA button.
  • Starter £3,000 — small businesses, new brands. One core service. Foundation-level delivery.
  • Growth £5,000 — businesses ready to scale. Two combined services. Integrated delivery.
  • Premium £10,000 — established businesses. Full-stack: design + development + marketing. Highlighted as "Most Popular."
  • Enterprise £20,000 — large projects. Bespoke scope. Dedicated team. Ongoing management.
Section 3
Interactive Configurator
"They'll recommend the right package for me in 60 seconds."
5-step quiz in a dark panel with progress bar:
What do you need?
A new website
Marketing & advertising
Creative / design work
A full campaign

Steps: What do you need → Business stage → Timeline → Budget range → Specific services (multi-select).

Result: recommended tier with reasoning, what's included, and "Get a Proposal for This Package →" CTA that links to Contact with the package pre-selected.
The configurator does three things simultaneously — helps the visitor, qualifies the lead, and demonstrates BA Digi's UX capability. "If they built this quiz this well, imagine what they'd build for my website."
Section 4
What's Always Included
4 cards: Dedicated point of contact · Clear project timeline · Full documentation · Post-delivery support. Standards that never change regardless of tier.
Section 5
FAQ
Accordion — 5–6 questions: Can I customise? What if my needs don't fit a tier? Typical timelines? Payment terms? Post-delivery support? Can I add services later?
Section 6
CTA
"Pick a package or get a custom proposal."
04
Contact

The simplest page. The selling is done. This page just needs to not get in the way.

Section 1
Hero
"Let's Talk About Your Project." — "Whether you're ready to start or just exploring — we're here."
Section 2
Contact Layout
Two-column layout (desktop), stacked (mobile):

Left — Contact info: Email (clickable), Phone (tap-to-call), WhatsApp (prominent green button), Location/timezone.

Right — Quick form: Name, Email, What do you need? (dropdown), Budget range (dropdown), Brief message, Submit button (copper).

If the visitor came through the configurator, the "What do you need?" and "Budget range" are pre-filled from their quiz answers.
Section 3
Closing Note
"Prefer to talk? Call us directly or message on WhatsApp. No contact forms required." — Multiple paths, zero friction.
